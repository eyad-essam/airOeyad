About EgyptAir

EgyptAir is the national flag carrier of the Arab Republic of Egypt and one of the oldest airlines in the world. Established in 1932 as Misr Airwork, it began its first commercial flights between Cairo and Alexandria in 1933, making it the first airline in the Middle East and Africa. Over the decades, the company went through several transformations, including a period during World War II when it was nationalized, and a later merger in the 1960s with Syrian Airlines under the name United Arab Airlines. In 1971, it officially returned to the name EgyptAir, which has since become a well-recognized brand across the globe.

Today, EgyptAir connects Egypt with over seventy destinations across Africa, Europe, Asia, and the Americas. The airline is known not only for its role in connecting Egypt to the world but also for symbolizing national pride. In 2008, it joined Star Alliance, allowing passengers to access a much wider network and benefit from international partnerships. EgyptAir has also invested in customer service, digital booking systems, and improved passenger experiences to keep up with global standards. Its hospitality is often described as warm and distinctly Egyptian, reflecting the culture of the country itself. With over ninety years of history, EgyptAir continues to represent resilience, growth, and ambition.
